Introduction and Course Learning Objectives

Epidemiology Terms

Be Able To:

  • Define basic terms in epidemiology.

There are a few basic terms in epidemiology that this learning module will make use of:

  • Latent Period: The time between the infection of an individual and that individual being able to transmit the disease to another person.

  • Infectious Period: The time during which an infected individual can transmit (spread) the disease to another non-infected individual.

  • Susceptible Period: The time during which an individual is not infected with the disease and can still be infected by it (has no immunity to infection).

  • Recovered Period: The time during which an individual, who was previously infected, shows no more symptoms and is immune to further infection by the same disease.

  • Incubation Period: The time between the infection of an individual and that individual showing symptoms of infection; also known as the “asymptomatic” period, as the infected individual during this time experiences no outward sign of infection.

  • Disease Period: The time during which an infected individual exhibits symptoms of the disease.

  • Serial Interval: The average length of time between an individual being infected and them infecting another individual, including the intermediate time it takes for vector transmission to occur.
